The Reality

South Sudan is at the Frontlines of the Climate Crisis.

Despite contributing minimally to global emissions, South Sudan faces some of the most severe consequences of climate change. Over consecutive years, historic flooding along the White Nile and unpredictable droughts have displaced millions, destroyed agriculture, and threatened rural livelihoods.

Climate action is no longer a luxury; it is a necessity for survival. By focusing on sustainable practices, community resilience, and environmental protection, we can mitigate these impacts.

Our Mission

Strategic Objectives

🌍

Raise Awareness

Educating local communities across all 10 states about climate change impacts and resilience strategies.

🌱

Protect Wetlands

Preserving the Sudd wetlands, vital ecosystems that balance the local climate and support biodiversity.

🌳

Reforestation

Combating rapid deforestation by distributing indigenous tree seedlings to rural communities.

☀️

Clean Energy

Advocating for solar power adoption to reduce reliance on diesel generators and charcoal.

🌾

Food Security

Introducing climate-smart agriculture techniques to ensure food production during extreme weather.

⚖️

Policy Support

Working with the government to enforce environmental laws and secure climate financing.
