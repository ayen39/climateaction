Problem Statement

The Reality of Climate Change in South Sudan

Our nation is increasingly affected by devastating flooding, prolonged droughts, and rapid deforestation. These are not just weather changes—they are threats to our very existence.

"These changes damage agriculture, reduce food production, and threaten livelihoods, especially in our rural communities."

The unpredictability of weather patterns has made traditional farming nearly impossible, leading to food insecurity for millions of our citizens.

80%

Of South Sudanese rely on agriculture that is now threatened by climate shifts.

Strategic Objectives

Our roadmap to a sustainable and climate-resilient South Sudan.

Raise Awareness

Educating the public about climate change and its severe local impacts to inspire mass action.

Environmental Protection

Promoting strategies to safeguard our unique ecosystems and wildlife from degradation.

Tree Planting

Aggressive afforestation and reforestation to restore our carbon sinks and natural canopy.

Reduce Pollution

Halt waste burning and curb deforestation to clean our air and preserve the soil.

Sustainable Living

Teaching communities how to use resources efficiently to harmonize with nature.

Support Policies

Advocating for environmental laws that protect natural resources for the next generation.

Daily Tips

Be Part of the Solution

Stop Waste Burning

Burning waste releases toxic chemicals. Instead, practice proper sorting and community composting.

Conserve Water

Water is precious during droughts. Use simple rainwater harvesting techniques for your garden.

Educate Others

Awareness is the first step. Talk to your family and neighbors about protecting our land.
